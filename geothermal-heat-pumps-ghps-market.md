<p><p>Geothermal heat pumps (GHPs) utilize the earth's stable temperature to heat and cool buildings efficiently. The market includes three primary types: Vertical closed loop systems, which use deep boreholes for heat exchange, making them suitable for limited space; Horizontal closed loop systems, which are installed in shallow trenches, ideal for larger areas; and Open loop systems, which draw groundwater directly for heat exchange, often requiring a reliable water source. Each type offers unique advantages based on site conditions.</p></p>
