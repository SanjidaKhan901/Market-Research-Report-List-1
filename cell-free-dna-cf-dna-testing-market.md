<p><p>The Cell-Free DNA (cf-DNA) testing market comprises three main types: donor-derived, circulating, and cell-free DNA. Donor-derived cf-DNA is obtained from organ or tissue transplant donors, primarily used for monitoring transplant acceptance. Circulating cf-DNA refers to fragments released into the bloodstream from healthy or diseased cells, utilized in cancer diagnostics and prenatal testing. The general cell-free DNA market encompasses both aforementioned categories, focusing on various applications such as disease detection, monitoring, and personalized medicine strategies.</p></p>
