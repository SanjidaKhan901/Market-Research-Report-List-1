<p><p>Thermal insulating coatings are specialized materials designed to reduce heat transfer, categorized by their temperature resistance. Coatings with less than 200℃ resistance are ideal for low-heat applications like pipelines or HVAC systems. Those with 200-600℃ resistance suit industrial equipment, boilers, and exhaust systems. Coatings rated 600-1000℃ are used in high-temperature environments such as furnaces or turbines. For extreme heat (1000-2000℃), these coatings are applied in aerospace, metallurgy, or refractory industries, ensuring durability and energy efficiency in extreme conditions.</p></p>
