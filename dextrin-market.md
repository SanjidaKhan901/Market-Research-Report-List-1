<p><p>The dextrin market primarily consists of two types: maltodextrin and cyclodextrin. Maltodextrin is a water-soluble carbohydrate used widely in the food industry for its thickening and stabilizing properties, often found in processed foods and beverages. Cyclodextrin, on the other hand, is characterized by its unique ring structure, enabling it to encapsulate flavors, fragrances, and active ingredients, making it valuable in pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and food applications. Both types cater to diverse industries, highlighting their functional versatility.</p></p>
