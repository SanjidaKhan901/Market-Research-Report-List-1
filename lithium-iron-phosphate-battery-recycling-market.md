<p><p>The Lithium Iron Phosphate (LiFePO4) battery recycling market has two primary types: echelon utilization and direct use. Echelon utilization involves repurposing used batteries for secondary applications, such as energy storage systems, where they can still provide value without needing complete refurbishment. In contrast, the direct use market focuses on recycling materials from spent batteries to create new batteries or other products. Both approaches promote sustainability and resource efficiency by maximizing the lifespan and utility of lithium iron phosphate batteries.</p></p>
