<p><p>The paneer market is segmented into four primary types based on texture and hardness. Soft paneer is creamy and easily crumbled, ideal for dishes like paneer tikka. Semi-soft paneer has a slightly firmer texture, suitable for grilling or sautéing. Medium-hard paneer offers a balanced density, often used in curries or as a salad ingredient. Hard paneer is dense and can be grated or used in cooking, making it popular for snacks or cooking applications. Each type caters to different culinary uses and consumer preferences.</p></p>
