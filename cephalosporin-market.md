<p><p>The cephalosporin market is categorized into four generations, each advancing in spectrum and efficacy. First-generation cephalosporins primarily target Gram-positive bacteria, widely used for skin and soft tissue infections. Second-generation cephalosporins expand to include some Gram-negative bacteria, effective for respiratory infections. Third-generation cephalosporins further enhance Gram-negative coverage, suitable for more severe infections, including hospital-acquired pathogens. Fourth-generation cephalosporins offer broad-spectrum activity against both Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria, making them ideal for treating resistant infections and complex cases.</p></p>
