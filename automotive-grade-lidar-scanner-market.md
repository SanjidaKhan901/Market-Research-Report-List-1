<p><p>The Automotive Grade LiDAR scanner market is primarily divided into two types: Mechanical LiDAR and Solid State LiDAR. Mechanical LiDAR uses rotating components to emit laser signals and capture 3D data, offering high resolution and range but with potential reliability concerns due to moving parts. Solid State LiDAR, on the other hand, relies on fixed components and electronic systems, providing a more compact, robust design with lower manufacturing costs and higher reliability, making it increasingly favored for automotive applications.</p></p>
