<p><p>The flavor enhancer market consists of various types, including Monosodium Glutamate (MSG), Hydrolyzed Vegetable Protein (HVP), and Yeast Extract. MSG is a widely used compound that intensifies savory flavors in food products. HVP is derived from protein hydrolysis, offering a rich umami taste and is often found in processed foods. Yeast Extract, made from yeast cells, adds depth to flavor profiles and is popular in soups and sauces. Together, these enhancers are integral in enhancing taste across various food applications.</p></p>
