<p><p>The battery electric car market primarily features two types: storage battery systems and fuel cells. Storage battery vehicles rely on rechargeable lithium-ion batteries to power electric motors, offering zero tailpipe emissions and high efficiency. Conversely, fuel cell electric vehicles (FCEVs) generate electricity through chemical reactions between hydrogen and oxygen, emitting only water vapor. Both technologies contribute to reducing greenhouse gas emissions and are central to the transition towards sustainable transportation, each addressing different consumer needs and infrastructure requirements.</p></p>
