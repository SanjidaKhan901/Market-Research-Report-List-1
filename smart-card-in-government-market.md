Contact smart cards require physical contact with a reader to access data, making them suitable for applications like ID cards and payment systems. In contrast, contactless smart cards use radio-frequency identification (RFID) technology, enabling data exchange without direct contact. This feature enhances convenience and speed in scenarios like public transportation and access control, offering robust security and efficiency for government services and citizen interactions.</p></p>
