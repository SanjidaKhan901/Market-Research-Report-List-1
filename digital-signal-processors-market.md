<p><p>The Digital Signal Processors (DSP) market can be categorized into two main types: programmable and non-programmable DSPs. Programmable DSPs offer flexibility, allowing developers to modify algorithms and processes as needed, making them suitable for applications requiring adaptability, such as telecommunications and audio processing. In contrast, non-programmable DSPs are designed for specific tasks, providing high efficiency and speed in fixed functions. These are typically used in consumer electronics and industrial applications where consistency and performance are paramount.</p></p>
