<p><p>The bioplastic packaging material market is primarily divided into two categories: biodegradable and non-biodegradable materials. Biodegradable materials, such as polylactic acid (PLA) and starch-based plastics, decompose naturally through microbial action, reducing environmental impact. In contrast, non-biodegradable bioplastics, like polyethylene made from renewable sources, offer durability and longevity but do not break down easily. Both types cater to diverse consumer needs, balancing sustainability with functionality, and are increasingly utilized across various industries to replace conventional plastic packaging.</p></p>
