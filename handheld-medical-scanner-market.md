<p><p>The handheld medical scanner market comprises two main types: wire and wireless. Wire handheld scanners rely on physical connections to transmit data, often offering stable performance but limited mobility. In contrast, wireless handheld scanners utilize Bluetooth or Wi-Fi technology, enhancing portability and convenience while providing flexibility in various healthcare settings. Both types serve different needs, with wire scanners typically used in stationary environments and wireless devices favored for dynamic, remote, or point-of-care applications, improving patient care and operational efficiency.</p></p>
