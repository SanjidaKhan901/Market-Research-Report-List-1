<p><p>The synthetic membranes market is primarily divided into two types: organic and inorganic membranes. Organic membranes, typically made from polymers, offer flexibility, ease of production, and cost-effectiveness, making them ideal for various applications, including water treatment and gas separation. In contrast, inorganic membranes, constructed from materials like ceramics or metals, provide superior thermal stability and chemical resistance, suitable for demanding environments like petrochemical processes and high-temperature applications. Each type serves distinct needs based on performance and durability requirements.</p></p>
