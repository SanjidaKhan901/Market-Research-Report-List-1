<p><p>The semiconductor plastics market encompasses various high-performance polymers utilized in electronic applications. Polyetheretherketone (PEEK) offers excellent thermal stability and chemical resistance, while Polyphenylene sulfide (PPS) is known for its rigidity and resistance to high temperatures. Polyethylene terephthalate (PET) is commonly used for its good mechanical properties. Polycarbonate (PC) is favored for its transparency and impact resistance. Polyoxymethylene (POM-C) has low friction and high stiffness. Polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) provides non-stick properties, and Polyimide (PI) and Polyetherimide (PEI) are recognized for thermal stability, while PAI (polyamide-imide) combines toughness with high-temperature performance.</p></p>
