<p><p>The surgery electrodes market encompasses several types, each designed for specific procedures. Needle electrodes are thin, pointed, and ideal for precise, minimally invasive surgeries, facilitating targeted tissue excision. Blade electrodes feature sharp edges for more extensive cuts, commonly used in open surgeries. Ball electrodes, shaped like a sphere, are effective for coagulation and cutting larger areas, while hook electrodes allow for grasping and cutting simultaneously, often utilized in laparoscopic procedures. Each type serves unique surgical needs, enhancing efficiency and outcomes.</p></p>
